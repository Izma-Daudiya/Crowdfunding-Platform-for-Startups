{% comment %} General Questions {% endcomment %}

What is Qrowd?

Qrowd is an equity-based crowdfunding platform that connects startups with investors. Startups can raise capital by offering equity stakes, and investors can invest in early-stage companies with high growth potential.

How does Qrowd work?

Startups first register startup on Qrowd by providing business plan, financial statements and pitch deck. Qrowd approves startup and after approval startup can start their campaign. Investors browse these campaigns and invest in exchange for equity. Once the funding goal is met, the startup receives the funds, and investors become shareholders.

Who can use Qrowd?

Startups: Early-stage companies looking to raise capital.
Investors: Accredited or non-accredited individuals interested in investing in startups.

{% comment %} Backers {% endcomment %}

For Backers:

Who can invest on Qrowd?

Both accredited and non-accredited investors can participate, depending on local regulations.

What is the minimum investment amount?

The minimum investment amount varies by campaign but is typically low to allow for broad participation.

How do I evaluate startups on Qrowd?

Qrowd approves startup based on the their business plan, financial statements and pitch deck. Qrowd provides detailed profiles of startups including pitch deck. Investors should conduct their own due diligence before investing.

What are the risks of investing in startups?

Investing in startups is high-risk and illiquid. There is a possibility of losing your entire investment if the startup fails. Diversification is recommended to mitigate risks.

How do I receive returns on my investment?

Returns are typically realized through equity appreciation, dividends, or an exit event such as an acquisition or IPO. However, these outcomes are not guaranteed.

{% comment %} Entrepreneurs {% endcomment %}

For Entrepreneurs:

How can my startup apply to raise funds on Qrowd?

Startups can apply by submitting an online application on the Qrowd platform. The application typically includes details about the business, team, financials, and funding goals.

What are the eligibility criteria for startups?

Startups must meet specific criteria, such as having a viable business model, a clear growth strategy, and a minimum level of traction or prototype development.

What fees does Qrowd charge startups?

Qrowd charges a success fee from startup after the funds are raised and also include platform fees or payment processing fees.

How long does it take to raise funds on Qrowd?

The duration varies, but campaigns typically run for 90 days. The timeline depends on the startup's ability to attract investors and meet its funding goal.

What happens if my startup doesn’t meet its funding goal?

If the funding goal isn’t met, the funds are returned to the investors, and the startup does not receive any capital.

How are investors kept informed about the startup’s progress?

Startups are required to provide regular updates and key milestones, to their investors through the Qrowd platform.

{% comment %} Legal & Compliance {% endcomment %}

Legal and Compliance:

How does Qrowd ensure compliance?

Qrowd adheres to local equity crowdfunding regulations and conducts due diligence on startups to ensure they meet platform standards.

Is my investment protected?

Investments in startups are not protected by insurance or government guarantees. Investors assume all risks associated with the investment.

What legal documents are involved?

Investors and startups sign legal agreements, such as a subscription agreement and shareholders’ agreement, outlining the terms of the investment.
